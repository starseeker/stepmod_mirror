Annex F
(informative)

Application activity model

The application activity model (AAM) is provided as an aid to understanding the scope and information requirements defined in this application protocol. The model is presented as a set of figures that contain the activity diagrams and a set of definitions of the activities and their data. Activities and data flows that are out of scope are marked with an asterisk.

The viewpoint is that of a design engineer.

F.1 Application activity model definitions

The following terms are used in the application activity model. Terms marked with an asterisk are outside the scope of this application protocol.

The definitions given in this annex do not supersede the definitions given in the main body of the text.

F.1.1 Administrative data

data associated with the design of the product that originates from design management

EXAMPLE    Examples of this type of data are company name, supplier code, security classification and contract number.

F.1.2 Analysis and test data*

data created during the design process and used to evaluate the design

F.1.3 Analysis and test models*

representations of the product design data used to evaluate the design

F.1.4 Analyze and test design*

the process of assessing the product's design against its requirements

F.1.5 Assembly layout

a representation of how individual parts fit together to make assemblies

F.1.6 Assembly requirements

constraints on the product design that pertain to the way the parts fit together to make an assembly

F.1.7 Change request disposition

a document describing the formal engineering response to a request for change to the engineering design

F.1.8 Company requirements*

those managerial decisions that place constraints on the operations of the company, that give direction or emphasis on areas for development, or that dictate decisions outside the local decision making paths These include the embodiment of policies and regulations that govern the operations of a company.

F.1.9 Conceptual design layout

a representation of how design ideas are visualized to become a product

F.1.10 Conceptualize product  

the activity in which the feasibility of the design of the major parts of the product is verified

F.1.11 Conceptualize product design*

the process of taking design ideas, requirements and design constraints, and turning them into a formalized description of the design

NOTE    This comprises conceptual design layout and assembly requirements.

F.1.12 Configuration control feedback*

deviations from standards, practices and procedures that relate to the engineering design approval process

F.1.13 Configuration controlled product design data

product design data logged into the formal configuration control system

F.1.14 Contractual requirements*

items specified in a legal document relating directly to product design

F.1.15 Control product design data*

formal product identifiers, such as part number and part version, that ensures conformance with established requirements, standards, practices and procedures

F.1.16 Define and formulate product design

the process of synthesizing a product design

NOTE    This process, which includes conceptual, preliminary and detailed design, is accomplished by theoretical analysis, design simulations and alternate design evaluations.

F.1.17 Design change request

a formal request to change any form, fit or function aspect of an existing design

F.1.18 Design concept*

a formal expression of the design ideas for the product

F.1.19 Design constraints*

restrictions that influence the creation of the design

F.1.20 Design feedback (design alternatives)*

analysis of previous or alternate designs used to formulate a new design

F.1.21 Design ideas*

the conceptualization of form, fit and function requirements for a suggested product design

F.1.22 Design integration structure

the representation of how multiple assemblies and subassemblies can be brought together to form functional systems

F.1.23 Develop concept  

the activity in which initial ideas to include new technologies, functional aspects of the product, possible materials and production processes are elaborated

F.1.24 Develop product  

the act of using tools and personnel to establish product requirements based on company requirements and standards, practices and procedures

F.1.25 Distribution request*

the formal document asking for copies of the product design to be forwarded to the appropriate personnel in an organization

F.1.26 Distribution tracking information

the data that identifies organizations or individuals who received some quantity of the released engineering product design data

F.1.27 Drawing corrections*

drawing changes based on standard drafting practices within a particular organization

F.1.28 Engineer product  

the activity in which the functionality, geometry, and technological properties of the product is defined

F.1.29 Engineering change authorization

an engineering document that identifies the design being released or changed

NOTE    It specifies what is to be done to the design and authorizes the design change activity.

F.1.30 Establish product requirements*

the activity that captures the product requirements

F.1.31 Establishing product requirements*

the process of establishing the criteria for the product that includes mission, objectives, costs, functions and market appeal

F.1.32 Existing product information

official design information about a product

F.1.33 Inspection, process, material requirements*

inspection requirements, processes done and material characteristics required to satisfy the product's designed form, fit and function

F.1.34 Licensed technology*

patented or proprietary processes or design information purchased or licensed from an outside source, such as a supplier or fabricator

F.1.35 Life-cycle requirements*

information required by the design that effect supportability, manufacturability and maintainability

F.1.36 Manage project*

the process of planning, scheduling and controlling other project activities to meet the project objectives

F.1.37 Management directives, schedule, budget*

the result of the management planning activities that effect the product design

F.1.38 Personnel and tools*

the personnel and tools from the contractor, customer or supplier

F.1.39 Product configuration identifiers

identifiers for the approved design which indicate its usage within the product structure

EXAMPLE    part number, effectivity configuration item.

F.1.40 Product data*

information about the product

F.1.41 Product design data

information produced by the design process that defines the form, fit and function of a product

F.1.42 Product development  

the activity in which a representation of the form, fit and function of a product is created, analyzed, tested and released

F.1.43 Product requirements*

criteria that specifies the functionality and characteristics of the product that includes mission, costs, functions, market appeal

F.1.44 Product structure

the relationship of the items to each other within the definition of a product

F.1.45 Project directives*

documents that describes project policy such as revisions to standard techniques, customer directions and special case requirements

F.1.46 Project requirements*

the criteria derived from the establishing product requirements activity that provide mission statements, budgets, and other data related to managing the project

F.1.47 Re-analyze and evaluate product  

the activity in which tests are executed

F.1.48 Release status

provides the information as to when, what and to whom data has been disseminated

F.1.49 Released engineering product design data

the engineering approved product design data that has been released under the control of an appropriate engineering authority

F.1.50 Research papers*

any documentation which describes processes or innovations uncovered due to the development of the product design

EXAMPLE    trade studies, lessons learned, patent descriptions.

F.1.51 Specifications, standards, practices and procedures

formalized documentation that influences the design activity to ensure commonality between products

F.1.52 Standard part information

product design data from standards organizations

F.1.53 Sythesize and refine configuration

the process of ensuring the form, fit and function from one sectionof the design into the overall product

F.1.54 Tool requirements*

the tooling required to support the testing, manufacturing and fabrication of a product design
